How can I see more details on my shifts and individual trips and deliveries?
Use the Activities section inside the Earnings tab to get more insights into your shifts and the jobs you worked

Want to take a closer look at the gig jobs you took on a particular day? Look no closer than the Activities section inside in the Earnings tab.

Whether you're in the day, week, month, or year view, as long as you look at a period where you did at least one gig job, then you will see the Activities section.

You will see a few different things on this page, let's walk through them one-by-one:

Engaged Time and Mileage Breakdowns

You will see two pie charts, which will show how much of your total work hours and your total work mileage that you were actually being paid for.

In other words, it shows your engaged hours and mileage as a percentage of total work.

Many gig apps will show you engaged hours and mileage while not showing you all the non-engaged hours and mileage that you had to put in to get paid.

If you think about all of the time that you had to commute to the area you were working in, when you're waiting for new job requests, when you're getting gas, or even when you're running to the bathroom -- this is work you have to do that you don't get paid for.

Viewing Activities for a Day

By clicking on the edit my work time link, you can edit your shifts. This is sometimes helpful in case you forgot to turn off your Driver's Seat shift tracking on or off. Read more about editing your shifts here.

Other Payments

This section tells you if you received any payments from gig apps that wasn't associated with any particular job or work. You might receive these Other Payments if you got a bonus after completing a certain number of jobs, for example. If the gig apps make adjustments to your pay from a previous day, the adjustment may also appear in this section.

Viewing your Work Blocks

This section will show a collection of your Work Blocks performed throughout the day. Each Work Block will include a timetable of when you were performing specific jobs for gig apps, as well as when you were not engaged on any particular job.

In addition, if you have tracked your Work Block from within Driver's Seat, you will see the miles you drove during that Work Block. The green-dot indicator tells you how good the mileage estimate is for that particular Work Block.

If the indicator is low, remember to turn on Driver's Seat shift tracking during your work times.

By clicking into any one of the individual jobs that you performed for a gig app, you will be able to see details on that trip, such as your Pay, your Tips, the time the job was Requested by a customer, and the Start and Stop Times.
